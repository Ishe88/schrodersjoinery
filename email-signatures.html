Implementation & Installation Guide

Most email clients let you copy the formatted signature visual directly from the page and paste it into your settings box. The Copy Signature button does exactly this automatically.


  1. Select your name and your preferred style (Option A or Option B).
  2. Click the gold Copy Signature button. This copies the signature as formatted rich text.
  3. Open your email client (Outlook, Gmail, or Apple Mail) and navigate to Signature Settings.
  4. Click into the text box where you type your signature, and press Ctrl + V (Windows) or Cmd + V (Mac) to paste it.
  5. Save changes.
Note for Outlook Desktop: Pasting directly preserves all alignments, colors, tables, and borders. The logo will load from the web when you compose emails.
  1. Open your Gmail inbox and click the Settings Cog in the top right, then select See all settings.
  2. Scroll down to the Signature section.
  3. Click + Create New and name it.
  4. Paste the signature using the Copy Signature button and hitting Ctrl + V / Cmd + V inside the signature field.
  5. Make sure to select your new signature under Signature defaults for both "For new emails use" and "On reply/forward use".
  6. Scroll to the bottom of Gmail Settings and click Save Changes.
  1. In Outlook, click File > Options > Mail > Signatures...
  2. Click New to create a signature name.
  3. Click into the Edit signature rich-text box and press Ctrl + V to paste the signature you copied via the Copy Signature button.
  4. Under Choose default signature, link the signature to your email account for New Messages and Replies/Forwards.
  5. Click OK to save.
Outlook Pro-Tip: If you want the most robust Outlook installation, click the Copy HTML Source button, save it as a .htm file inside your Outlook Signatures directory (usually located at %APPDATA%\Microsoft\Signatures), and Outlook will load the file natively.
